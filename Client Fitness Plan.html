Training Tips
How to train · Not just what to do
📈
Progressive Overload — The Most Important Rule
When you hit the top of the rep range on every set with good form, increase the weight by the smallest available increment next session. Example: Lat Pulldown 3×12 at 40kg → next session try 42.5kg. Without this, results stop.
⬇️
Slow Negatives (Eccentric)
The lowering phase of every rep should be controlled — 2–3 seconds down. The explosive phase is up. This applies to every exercise. Slow negatives cause more muscle damage and produce more growth. Never drop the weight or let gravity do the work.
🎯
RIR — Reps in Reserve
Compound lifts (squat, press, row, deadlift): Stop with 1–3 reps left in the tank. Isolation exercises (curls, raises, pushdowns): Stop with 0–2 reps left. Beginners often train either too easy or dangerously hard — RIR keeps intensity in the right zone.
🔗
Mind-Muscle Connection
On every isolation exercise — curls, raises, pushdowns — think about the muscle you are trying to work, not the weight. Before each set, squeeze the target muscle. During the rep, feel it contract. This is especially important as a beginner before heavy weights are possible.
😤
Compound vs Isolation — Different Goals
Compound lifts (bench, squat, row, press) — these build overall strength and burn the most calories. Go heavier, fewer reps (8–12). Isolation exercises (curls, lateral raises, pushdowns) — these shape specific muscles. Go lighter, more reps (12–15), feel the muscle work.
⏱️
Rest Between Sets
Compound lifts: 90 seconds minimum. Isolation exercises: 60 seconds. Resting too short reduces performance in the next set. Resting too long wastes time and reduces workout density. Use a timer.
🏁
Warm Up Properly
5 minutes incline treadmill before every session. Then for the first exercise of each muscle group — do 1 light warm-up set at 50% of working weight before your working sets. Never start a heavy compound cold. Joint injuries from skipping warm-up are common at his weight.
📝
Track Every Session
Phone notes — date, exercise, weight, reps for every set. The goal every session is to beat last week's numbers by even one rep or 2.5kg. Without tracking you cannot do this. Gut feeling is not reliable.
😰
First 3 Weeks Will Be Brutal
Severe muscle soreness (DOMS) is expected and normal. Do not skip sessions because of soreness — training through it with lighter weights actually speeds recovery. Soreness virtually disappears after week 3–4 as the body adapts.
🚫
Form Before Weight — Always
At 115kg, adding weight too fast to compound lifts is how injuries happen. If form breaks during a set — back is rounding on RDL, knees caving on squat — reduce weight immediately. Ego lifting at his weight leads to serious knee, back, and shoulder injuries that derail months of progress.

Plateau Protocol
What to do when progress stalls
Before declaring a plateau — confirm adherence is actually consistent. Most "plateaus" are caused by inconsistent tracking or hidden calories, not metabolic adaptation.
Calorie Adjustment Decision Tree
Every 2–3 Weeks
✅
Losing 0.5–1% of bodyweight per week
Do nothing. Plan is working. Do not adjust. At 115kg this means 0.5–1.1kg per week — this is the target zone.
⚠️
Losing too fast — more than 1% for several weeks
Add 100–200 kcal per day. Too fast means muscle loss. Add carbs first — extra rice or oats portion. Do not add junk food.
🛑
Plateau — no change in 7-day average for 2 consecutive weeks with confirmed good adherence
Option A: Reduce calories by 100–150 kcal/day (remove one small carb portion). Option B: Add 20 minutes of weekly walking. Start with Option A first. Only do one change at a time.
📋
Before blaming the plan — check adherence first
Was protein hit every day? Were meals skipped? Was cold coffee with sugar still happening? Were workouts consistent? Most plateaus are adherence problems not metabolic ones.

Never Do These When Stuck
Common Mistakes
✗
Do not drop below 1800 kcal
Especially with thyroid family history. Too aggressive causes muscle loss and metabolic adaptation that makes future fat loss harder.
✗
Do not dramatically increase cardio
Adding hours of cardio causes fatigue, increases hunger, and eats muscle. Small increments only — 20 minutes per week maximum increase.
✗
Do not change multiple things at once
Change one variable at a time — either calories or cardio, never both simultaneously. Otherwise you cannot tell which change caused which result.
✗
Do not declare a plateau after one week
A true plateau is 2 consecutive weeks of no movement in the 7-day average with confirmed adherence. One week of stalled weight is normal fluctuation.

From Your Coach
The things that actually matter · Read this first
🔱
1 — Discipline, Consistency, Drive
These are the three secret sauces. No supplement, no program, no coach can replace them. Motivation gets you started. Discipline keeps you going when motivation disappears. And it will disappear. That is when the real work begins. Show up anyway.
📊
2 — Numbers Don't Lie
Track your workouts. Track your sleep. Track your food. You will know exactly what happened last time, what is going to happen today, and what will happen later. Gut feeling is not data. Data is data. The people who track are the people who progress.
🎧
3 — Learn to Train Without Music and Without Caffeine
This one is controversial. But if you can only train well with music and pre-workout, you are dependent on external stimulation to perform. Train without them sometimes. Build the internal drive. When you can push hard in silence on an empty stomach — that is real mental strength. The music and coffee become a bonus, not a requirement.
💪
4 — A Bad Workout Is Better Than a Skipped Workout
This is the most important one. You will have days where you are tired, unmotivated, sore, and drained. Go anyway. A half-effort session still builds the habit. Still sends the signal to your body. Still beats staying home. Missing one session makes missing the next one easier. Showing up makes showing up easier.
🌱
5 — Trust the Process and Keep It Sustainable
The more you trust the process and the more you keep everything sustainable, the more progress you are going to make. Extreme diets and extreme training produce extreme burnout. This plan is built for the long game — 10 to 14 months of consistent work that compounds into a completely different body and life.
🪞
6 — Stop Comparing. Start Competing With Yourself.
Social media is a highlight reel. The person you see with the shredded physique has been training for years, not months. Your only competition is who you were last week. Did you lift more? Sleep better? Eat cleaner? That is the only comparison that matters.
🍽️
7 — Food Is Fuel, Not a Reward or Punishment
You are not eating clean to punish yourself for being overweight. You are not rewarding yourself with junk food after a good session. Food is information your body uses to recover, build muscle, and burn fat. Change the relationship with food and everything else becomes easier.
📸
8 — Take Progress Photos, Not Just Scale Readings
The scale will lie to you regularly. Water retention, muscle gain, and food timing all affect it daily. A photo every 4 weeks in the same lighting and pose tells the real story. Most people who quit do so because they only watched the scale. The people who stay do so because they can see what is actually changing.
😴
9 — Sleep Is the Most Underrated Tool in This Plan
You do not grow in the gym. You grow when you sleep. Growth hormone is released during deep sleep — at 16, this is at its lifetime peak. Every hour of sleep you cut is a direct reduction in how much fat you lose and how much muscle you build. Protect your sleep like you protect your training.
